Walid Arfush

International
media manager

Walid Arfush is an international media manager who served for over 10 years at Euronews TV channel.

He created SN Media which focused on strategic communications, media relations, crisis communications, intelligence services, and reputation management.

Walid was born in northern Lebanon in 1971. He spent most of his childhood and young age in civil war, before leaving for France and Soviet Ukraine to continue his studies in journalism, without getting to finish it due to early work engagements.

A pioneer in media, he created in 1995 one of the first FM radio stations (Super Nova Radio) in independent Ukraine; soon to become the voice of RFI ( Radio France International ) in Ukraine’s capital Kyiv; airing news in different languages including French English and Arabic

  • Due to his popularity among the youth, he was appointed as the first UN Goodwill Ambassador for Aids issues.

    The creative works of his PR agency (Super Nova) made it to the headquarters of the UN in New York with a message for youth to “Rule your Life” as a main instrument to get protection against Aids.

  • His humanitarian work led him to create an anti-racism association “SOS! Racism!” and lobbied for a law in the Ukrainian Parliament that placed racism as a criminal act.

    In 2010 he was appointed by the Ukrainian government as vice president of the National TV of Ukraine, where he was in charge of entertainment & international affairs. And he introduced the public vote to elect the representative of Ukraine for the well-known musical contest Eurovision.

  • On international affairs, Walid successfully introduced the Ukrainian language to Euronews, and then, created a full version of Euronews in Ukrainian.

    In 2014 Walid was appointed as head of CIS countries at Euronews.

    In 2019 he was appointed vice president in charge of development at Euronews International.

  • Since the beginning of the full-scale war by Russia against Ukraine in 2022, Walid is more focused on assisting Ukrainian refugees in France.

    He’s based in Ukraine and France and has a long work history with Kazakhstan, a country that he sees as the new Europe in Central Asia.

    While not working on media and humanitarian efforts, he practices yoga daily.
